Getting Started

An overview of GroupFuel, how to download, functionalities and everything you need to know in order to track and manage your fuel consumption smarter!

Web App Guide

Create An Account

Create an account by clicking the "Create Account" button that is placed in the upper right hand of the page, if you already have an account, just click login and enter your details.

Manage Your Cars

Click Manage Account->Manage Cars in order to go to the screen that shows you all the raw details about cars you own and cars that you are listed as a driver in.

If you wish to remove a car from the Cars I Own list, just click the red Remove Car button, similar action can be done if you wish to remove yourself as a driver from a car by clicking Leave Car button. Want to add a car to your list? Just click the Add a car button which will open a pop up that will require you to fill in the necessary information about the car, please make sure you don't miss any field.

Want to see the drivers of a car and\or add a driver to one of your cars? Just click the green Driver Details button of the car, a popup will pop on your screen with the details. Easily add or remove driver by clicking Add or Remove Driver buttons.

Manage Your Account

Manage your account in the personal information section. Simply click Manage Account -> Edit Profile and update your information if needed.

Statistics (Or: GroupFuel - The Good Parts)

We at GroupFuel provide 2 different kinds of statistics, those who are relevant to your cars usage and those that are global. If you click My Usage, you will be directed into a page that displays a variety of different statistics and interesting information regarding your fuel usage. You can see a table named Cars Usage that presents the most up to date data of your cars, a table named Fuel History which is basically a log of all the recent fuelings of your cars. You can see in the right hand side of the page two types of charts - Pie Charts and Line charts that presents graphic data about your cars fuel usage. In the Pie Charts Section you can choose between the following charts: Fuel Per Car, Km per Liter and Money per Car. In the Line Charts Section you can choose between the following types of data: Fuel per month and Money per month. And lastly, in the lower part of the page there is Google Maps map with red pins on it that represents the location of your cars' fuelings, making it easier for you to control integrity of driving locations.

If you want to see general data, what we call "global data", meaning not just about your cars, just click Stats, choose the model that you want to get data about (same as in add a car part) and click submit.
